Here are some exercises, quizzes, mini-projects, and real-world problems related to machine learning:

**Exercises:**

1. **Linear Regression**: Given a dataset of exam scores and hours studied, use linear regression to predict the score of a student who studied for 5 hours.
	* Dataset: [(2, 90), (4, 80), (6, 70), (8, 60), (10, 50)]
	* Answer: Use the formula for linear regression: y = mx + b, where m is the slope and b is the intercept. Calculate the slope and intercept using the given data points.
	* Explanation: The slope (m) represents the change in score for a one-hour increase in study time. The intercept (b) represents the score when the student studies for 0 hours.
2. **Decision Trees**: Given a dataset of customer information (age, income, credit score), use a decision tree to classify a new customer as either "high risk" or "low risk" for a loan.
	* Dataset: [(25, 50000, 700), (30, 60000, 800), (35, 70000, 900), (40, 80000, 1000)]
	* Answer: Create a decision tree with the following rules:
		+ If age < 30, then low risk
		+ If income < 60000, then high risk
		+ If credit score < 800, then high risk
	* Explanation: The decision tree uses a series of if-then statements to classify the customer based on their characteristics.
3. **Clustering**: Given a dataset of customer purchase history, use k-means clustering to group customers into three clusters based on their spending habits.
	* Dataset: [(100, 200, 300), (200, 300, 400), (300, 400, 500), (400, 500, 600)]
	* Answer: Use the k-means algorithm to group the customers into three clusters:
		+ Cluster 1: (100, 200, 300), (200, 300, 400)
		+ Cluster 2: (300, 400, 500)
		+ Cluster 3: (400, 500, 600)
	* Explanation: The k-means algorithm groups similar customers together based on their spending habits.

**Quizzes:**

1. What is the primary difference between supervised and unsupervised learning?
	* A) Supervised learning uses labeled data, while unsupervised learning uses unlabeled data.
	* B) Supervised learning uses unlabeled data, while unsupervised learning uses labeled data.
	* C) Supervised learning is used for regression, while unsupervised learning is used for classification.
	* D) Supervised learning is used for classification, while unsupervised learning is used for regression.
	* Answer: A) Supervised learning uses labeled data, while unsupervised learning uses unlabeled data.
2. Which machine learning algorithm is commonly used for text classification?
	* A) Decision Trees
	* B) Random Forest
	* C) Support Vector Machines (SVM)
	* D) Naive Bayes
	* Answer: D) Naive Bayes
3. What is the purpose of regularization in machine learning?
	* A) To increase the complexity of the model
	* B) To decrease the complexity of the model
	* C) To improve the accuracy of the model
	* D) To reduce the computational cost of the model
	* Answer: B) To decrease the complexity of the model

**Mini-Projects:**

1. **Image Classification**: Use a convolutional neural network (CNN) to classify images into different categories (e.g. dogs, cats, cars).
	* Dataset: CIFAR-10
	* Task: Train a CNN to achieve an accuracy of at least 80% on the test set.
	* Explanation: The CNN uses convolutional and pooling layers to extract features from the images, and fully connected layers to classify the images.
2. **Natural Language Processing**: Use a recurrent neural network (RNN) to predict the next word in a sentence.
	* Dataset: 20 Newsgroups
	* Task: Train an RNN to achieve a perplexity of at least 100 on the test set.
	* Explanation: The RNN uses a sequence of words as input and predicts the next word in the sequence.
3. **Recommendation System**: Use a collaborative filtering algorithm to recommend movies to users based on their rating history.
	* Dataset: MovieLens
	* Task: Train a collaborative filtering algorithm to achieve a precision of at least 0.5 on the test set.
	* Explanation: The collaborative filtering algorithm uses the rating history of users to recommend movies that are likely to be of interest.

**Real-World Problems:**

1. **Predicting Customer Churn**: A telecom company wants to predict which customers are likely to churn (i.e. switch to a different provider) based on their usage patterns and demographic information.
	* Dataset: Customer usage data and demographic information
	* Task: Train a machine learning model to predict customer churn with an accuracy of at least 80%.
	* Explanation: The machine learning model uses the customer usage data and demographic information to predict the likelihood of churn.
2. **Detecting Credit Card Fraud**: A bank wants to detect credit card fraud based on transaction data.
	* Dataset: Transaction data (e.g. amount, location, time)
	* Task: Train a machine learning model to detect credit card fraud with an accuracy of at least 90%.
	* Explanation: The machine learning model uses the transaction data to detect patterns that are indicative of fraud.
3. **Predicting Stock Prices**: An investor wants to predict the stock price of a company based on historical data.
	* Dataset: Historical stock price data
	* Task: Train a machine learning model to predict the stock price with an accuracy of at least 80%.
	* Explanation: The machine learning model uses the historical stock price data to predict the future stock price.
